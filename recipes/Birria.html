Birria

A traditional and versatile Mexican dish.

This juicy authentic Mexican dish can be enjoyed in various way. Enjoyed as a stew or in cheesy quesadillas. The taste is incredible any way you choose.

Ingredients

  • 6-8 guallio chiles; remove stem and seeds
  • 3-4 Chile de arbol; remove stem and seeds
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Head of garlic; peeled
  • 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 small onion
  • 2 chiptole peppers in adobo sauce
  • 3 1/2 lb boneless beef chuck; trimmed and cut into cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 14 oz can whole tomatos
  • 1 stick Canela (Mexican Cinnamon)
  • 3 sprigs fresh marjom
  • 1 yellow onion, quartered
  • 5 bay leaves
  • 1 Tbsp black peppercorns
  • Corn tortillas
  • Oaxaca cheese (optional)
  1. Roughly rip the chiles into smaller pieces and add them to a medium saucepan along with the cumin seeds. Toast the chiles and cumin for 5 minutes until fragant but not smoking.
  2. Cover the chiles with about 2 cups of water and bring the water to a simmer. Cover the saucepan with a lid and turn off the heat, allow the mixture to steep for 10-20 minutes.
  3. Transfer the chile mixture(water included) to a blender. Add the garlic, oregano, cloves, onion, and peppers. Add enough water to cover all of the ingredients.
  4. Blend the mixture until completely smooth, reserve until use.
  5. Salt chuck pieces on a large sheet tray and sit at room temp for 40 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, place the oxtails in a roasting pan and toss with vegetable oil
  7. Roast the oxtails at 475 degrees F for 20 minutes.
  8. Pour the tomatos and the blended chile mixture over the roasted oxtails. Add enough water to come about halfway up the sides of the oxtails.
  9. Add the beef chuck, cinnamon stick, fresh marjoram, onion, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
  10. Braise beef at 350F for 3 hours until tender. Make sure to flip all of the pieces of meat halfway through the cook time and add more water if necessary.
  11. Remove the beef from the roasting pan and shred it with two forks and reserve.
  12. Strain the remaining braising liquid into a large bowl.
  13. Add about 1 cup of the braising liquid to the removed shredded beef.
  14. Cover the remaining braising liquid and the beef with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight.
  15. Remove the solidified fat from the top of the braising liquid and reheat it in a medium sized skillet.
  16. Reheat the braising liquid in a medium pot. Season with salt or pepper if necessary. This is your consome.
  17. Reheat the shredded beef in another medium pot.
  18. Preheat a large cast iron skillet. Warm one tortilla on each side.
  19. Dip the corn tortilla in the liquefied fat and transfer backt to skillet.
  20. Top the tortilla with shredded oaxaca cheese and and genours portion of beef. Fold in half, cook for 1 minute on each side. Repeat for al tortillas.
  21. Serve tacos with a cup of the consome, white onion, jalapeno peppers, limes, and cliantro.
